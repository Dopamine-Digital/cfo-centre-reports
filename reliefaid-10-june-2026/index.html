Foreign exchange and transfer leakage

Every dollar sent to five conflict zones crosses at least one FX conversion. Without a dedicated charity FX provider or multi-currency accounts, 3 to 8 percent of transferred funds is typically lost to bank margins and fees.

Revenue concentration and donor lifecycle

Income dropped 56% in two years with no visible recurring giving programme. Acquiring a new donor costs 5 to 10 times more than retaining an existing one. A structured monthly giving and donor retention system typically recovers a significant share of lapsed revenue.

Two consecutive deficit years totalling approximately $510K. Without a reserves policy and forward cash flow model, reactive cost cuts and missed crisis response windows quietly erode the organisation's long-term capacity to deliver aid.

Two-entity structure across NZ and Canada, Charities Commission annual returns, AML/CFT obligations for fund transfers to conflict zones. Without systematised processes, compliance work consumes founder and volunteer hours at a hidden cost that could go toward mission delivery.
